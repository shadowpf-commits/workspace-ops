# EA Market Profile — Japan & Korea

## Overview

East Asia (Japan + Korea) represents high-value, high-complexity markets with distinct platform ecosystems, user behaviors, and marketing channels. Taiwan is covered in `cn-tw-hk-market-profile.md`.

---

## ⚠️ Empirical Finding: MOBA Users Are Primary Target

**Based on prior UA tests, MOBA category users outperform other segments. However, in JP/KR, MOBA culture differs:**

- **KR:** Wild Rift audience = PRIMARY target (LoL heritage is strong)
- **JP:** NOT a traditional MOBA market — Hero Shooter / anime-FPS crossover is more relevant than pure MOBA
- Reference game audiences (Wild Rift KR) are the prime lookalike seeds for UA in KR only

## 🇯🇵 Japan (JP)

**Market Size & Growth**
- Population: 125M+
- Gaming market: $18B+ (3rd largest globally)
- Mobile gaming: ~60% of total gaming revenue
- Player spending per capita: Among highest in world

**User Profile**
- Age: 15-55, broad demographic including female gamers
- Play habits: Mobile-first; RPG, gacha, and puzzle games dominate
- Payment: High ARPU; credit card + carrier billing; very willing to spend
- Top games: Uma Musume, Monster Strike, Granblue Fantasy, Honor of Kings (CN-version plays in JP)

**Target Audience Sizing (MOBA-Shooter Type)**
- Core TA (active MOBA players): ~3-5M
- Secondary TA (FPS/TPS fans): ~2-4M
- Total Addressable (mobile action players): ~8-12M
- Reachable TA (with budget/media access): ~2-5M
- TA Profile: Anime-style game fans + competitive mobile gamers; 25-35 demographic important; female MOBA players exist but are underserved in West
- MOBA crossover rate: Estimated 15-25% (JP MOBA scene is smaller than SEA/LATAM; but quality over quantity)
- ⚠️ Note: JP is not a traditional MOBA market. Hero shooter crossover is more relevant than MOBA crossover. Target Valorant/Rivals-style audience more than ML/WR audience.

**Platform Distribution**
- Mobile: ~60%
- Console: ~25% (PlayStation dominant; Nintendo forcasual)
- PC: ~15%
- iOS: ~55% (JP has highest iOS ratio among major markets)
- Google Play: ~45%

**Channel Performance**
| Channel | Viability | Notes |
|---------|-----------|-------|
| Google UAC | ✅ Excellent | Primary performance channel |
| Apple Search Ads | ✅ Excellent | JP iOS users have highest LTV; very viable |
| Facebook/Meta | ⚠️ Limited | Low engagement in JP |
| TikTok | ✅ Strong | Younger audience; good for casual/mid-core |
| YouTube | ✅ Excellent | Gaming content consumption is massive |
| Twitter/X | ✅ Strong | Community, PR, and viral moments |
| LINE Ads | ✅ Essential | LINE messenger dominant; good for 25-45 demo |
| CyberAgent (CAd) | ✅ Strong | Japanese-native DSP; good for gaming |
| Twitter/X | ✅ Strong | Community building, events |

**Cultural Notes**
- Japan has unique gaming culture — gacha (loot box) is core monetization
- RPG and anime-style games dominate
- "Collector's mentality" is strong — completionists drive spending
- Social features differ from Western expectations (写信/friend letters, not FB friends)
- Honne (本音) vs Tatemae (建前) — players may not express true opinions publicly
- Quality expectations is extremely high — localization must be flawless

**Gacha Regulatory Status**
- Gacha/loot boxes are legal in Japan
- BUT there is social pressure and some self-regulation
- "Complete gacha" ( completto gacha) is banned — cannot require full set to unlock
- Must disclose odds (公示率) for paid gacha

**Gaming Media & KOL**
- YouTube is MASSIVE for game content
- Twitch is growing but YouTube dominant
- Key YouTubers: Many gaming influencers with millions of subscribers
- Niconico (ニコニコ) is culturally important but niche now
- Japanese gaming media: 4gamer, Game Spark, Famitsu

**Localization Requirements**
- Japanese (日本語) — extremely high quality expected
- Native speaker essential; machine translation is unacceptable
- Cultural adaptation: Anime tropes, honorifics (-san, -chan), seasonal references
- Kanji, Hiragana, Katakana — do not mix incorrectly
- Payment: Credit card + carrier billing (NTT, Softbank, AU)

## 🇰🇷 South Korea (KR)

**Market Size & Growth**
- Population: 52M+
- Gaming market: $8B+
- Mobile gaming: ~55% of total gaming revenue
- PC gaming is historically strong; KSF (PC bangs) culture important

**User Profile**
- Age: 18-45, predominantly male for mid-core/hardcore
- Play habits: Mobile + PC hybrid; esports viewership massive
- Payment: High ARPU; credit card, Samsung Pay; spend willingly
- Top games: League of Legends: Wild Rift, FIFA Online, Sudden Attack (突撃), MapleStory

**Target Audience Sizing (MOBA-Shooter Type)**
- Core TA (active MOBA players): ~4-6M
- Secondary TA (FPS/TPS fans): ~5-8M
- Total Addressable (mobile action players): ~12-18M
- Reachable TA (with budget/media access): ~3-8M
- TA Profile: Male 16-35 dominant; Wild Rift is the MOBA baseline; FPS is MASSIVE (Sudden Attack, Crossfire); KR esports culture = high engagement potential
- MOBA crossover rate (WR → MOBA-shooter): Estimated 35-45%
- Unique advantage: KR players are highly competitive; team-based shooters with ranking systems perform exceptionally

**Platform Distribution**
- Mobile: ~55%
- PC: ~35%
- Console: ~10%
- Google Play + One Store (한국형) dominant on Android
- iOS: ~30%

**Channel Performance**
| Channel | Viability | Notes |
|---------|-----------|-------|
| Google UAC | ✅ Excellent | Primary channel |
| Apple Search Ads | ✅ Strong | iOS users are high-value |
| Facebook/Meta | ⚠️ Limited | Low organic reach in KR |
| TikTok | ✅ Growing | Younger demographic (18-24) |
| Naver | ✅ Essential | Korea's dominant search engine (not Google!) |
| Kakao Ads | ✅ Strong | KakaoTalk messenger; 95%+ penetration |
| YouTube | ✅ Excellent | Gaming content, trailers |

**Platform Notes**
- One Store (원스토어): Korea's local Android store; required for full coverage
- Naver: Korea's #1 search engine; SEO/SEM critical, not Google
- KakaoTalk: Dominant messenger; Kakao Ads platform is essential

**Cultural Notes**
- Esports is mainstream culture — League of Legends, StarCraft heritage
- PC gaming culture is strong; PC bangs (internet cafes) still relevant
- "Kick" (킥) culture — intense, passionate gaming community
- Mobile game clans/guilds are social hubs
- Korean players are vocal about balance/patch issues
- Ranking and competitive status matter deeply

**Marketing Calendar**

| Dec 25 | Christmas | ⭐⭐⭐ | Holiday events |
| Lunar Dec 30-31 | New Year's Eve (설날) | ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ | Biggest holiday; massive spending |

**Gaming Media & KOL**
- AfreecaTV (아프리카TV): Korea's Twitch equivalent; gaming streamers are huge
- Naver Blog/Post: Important for PR and community
- YouTube: Gaming content is massive
- K-pop crossover: Gaming + idol collaborations work well

**Localization Requirements**
- Korean (한국어) — native quality essential
- Culturally resonant: Korean honorifics, clan/guild culture
- One Store integration for Android
- Local customer service in Korean
- Game client must be KR-region specific (data laws)
